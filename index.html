Why AI literacy, why now

Already in their world

Voice assistants, recommendation feeds, AI art generators — children use AI daily, often without knowing it. Guided exposure beats unguided exposure.

Curiosity, not coding

No keyboard-heavy syllabus, no pressure. Age-appropriate concepts, simple analogies, and a lot of asking "how does that work?"

Confidence, not fear

Children who understand how a tool works tend to trust it appropriately — neither blindly nor anxiously. That's the habit we're building.

Three tracks, built by age

Different ages learn differently — so the curriculum, pace, and projects change with them. Every track includes a Student Workbook, a Reading Book, and small-group online classes.

Explorers

First steps into AI

Grades 1–3

Understanding AI basics, spotting AI in everyday life, and learning smarter ways to think — through stories, play, and the "AI Tales" reading book.

Builders

AI in everyday life

Grades 4–5

How AI actually works, using real AI tools responsibly, writing better prompts, and implementing AI in school and daily tasks — with the "AI Chronicles" reading book.

Innovators

Pro-level AI skills

Grades 6–7

Intro to coding in Python and Scratch, how AI models are trained, machine learning, AI ethics, and a capstone project — with the "AI Frontiers" reading book.

Built for parents who are (rightly) careful

We built InfiniMind for the parent who isn't anti-technology — just unconvinced that "AI institute" and "eight-year-old" belong in the same sentence without real safeguards. So we made the safeguards the product, not an afterthought.

  • Small live groups — never unsupervised one-on-one chats between a child and a mentor
  • Every mentor is background-checked and trained specifically for young learners
  • Sit in on any class, anytime — no separate parent account required
  • We collect the minimum data needed to run a class, nothing more
  • No long-term contracts — leave whenever the fit isn't right
